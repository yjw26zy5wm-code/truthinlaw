What Is an AI Hallucination?

Artificial intelligence can write essays, answer questions, summarize court cases, and even draft legal documents.

But sometimes it produces information that is completely false while sounding 100% confident. This is known as an AI hallucination.

What is an AI hallucination?

An AI hallucination happens when an AI system generates information that appears accurate but is actually fabricated, incorrect, or unsupported by evidence. Unlike a human intentionally lying, the AI is predicting words rather than verifying facts.

Why does it happen?

  • AI predicts text instead of checking facts.
  • It may invent legal citations.
  • It can misunderstand context.
  • It sometimes fills missing information with guesses.

Why does it matter?

Incorrect AI-generated legal information can lead to serious consequences, including financial loss, court sanctions, and poor legal decisions. Always verify important information using official sources.

How can you protect yourself?

  • Verify legal citations.
  • Use official government websites.
  • Double-check AI answers.
  • Never rely on one AI response for legal advice.
